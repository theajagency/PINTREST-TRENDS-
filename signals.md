# How to Read Pinterest Trend Signals

Use these criteria to score each keyword as **UP**, **STABLE**, or **WEAK**.

---

## TRENDING UP
A keyword scores UP when multiple of the following are true:

- Pinterest boards have 900+ pins or ideas saved
- Multiple boards are dated 2026 (current year)
- Content from the current or previous month is prominent
- Pinterest auto-suggests many related terms for this keyword
- A wide variety of aesthetics and styles appear (not just one niche)
- New sub-terms are emerging (e.g., "coquette mini dress" branching off "mini dress")
- Search results show a mix of product pins, outfit shots, and inspo boards — all active

---

## STABLE
A keyword scores STABLE when:

- Consistent board presence with moderate saves (200–500+ pins)
- Content is not brand new but not outdated either
- Search results are solid but not surging
- It's a known seasonal term performing as expected for the time of year
- No dramatic increase or decrease in related suggestions

---

## WEAK / FADING
A keyword scores WEAK when multiple of the following are true:

- Few results, or results are dominated by old pins (pre-2025)
- Board counts are low (under 50–100 ideas)
- No new 2026-dated boards
- Pinterest suggests very few or no related terms
- Only one narrow aesthetic is showing up (overly niche)
- Seasonal mismatch (e.g., heavy winter content for a summer keyword)
- Results feel stale, repetitive, or low-engagement

---

## Related Term Signals
When Pinterest surfaces auto-suggested or related searches, record them. These are the most valuable signals — they show what users are actually typing and what's gaining momentum.

Format: `[searched keyword] → [related term Pinterest surfaced]`

Example:
- `floral dress → floral midi dress, cottage core floral dress, floral wrap dress`

If a related term appears across 3+ different keyword searches, flag it as an **emerging macro trend**.

---

## Confidence Notes
- Board count alone is not enough — check if the boards are recent and actively saved
- A keyword with 900 pins from 2022 is WEAK, not UP
- A keyword with 200 pins from March 2026 may be UP
- Trust recency over volume
